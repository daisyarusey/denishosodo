KEY ACHIEVEMENTS SO FAR!

The role of a leader includes many aspects of the job description, calendar of activities and engagements. But ultimately leaders are measured on outcomes, not activities. And leaders must also be candid on where more could have been done.

So I summarize here the visible significant outcomes, during my term of service to YOU, the member as:
  1. National Vice Chairman of ICPAK until 2019
  2. ICPAK Council Member from 2017 to 2019
Where I was
  • Convener of the Audit, Risk and Compliance Committee as well as the Internal Auditors Sub-committee from 2017-2019
  • Co-convener of the Practitioners Development Committee
  • Chair of the special committee in-charge of Implementation of amendments to the Accountants Acts in 2018-2019 which expanded the scope of Accountancy, offered new areas of practice to members and enabled multiple licensing
When you elected me into Council in 2017, this is what I promised to do;
  1. Defending the Credibility of the CPA Brand
  2. Empowering members of ICPAK
  3. Advocacy on Accountability matters in our Country

And my fellow Accountants, as part of the Council, it was in our time that the current Strategic plan that covers the period 2019- 2021 was developed and approved identifying the four (4) main areas of focus.

We achieved these outcomes for members:
  1. The Accountants Act was amended to expand the definition of Accountancy, secure new areas of growth opportunities for members and pave way for Multiple licenses
  2. The mandatory training curriculum for practitioners was started to safeguard Audit quality
  3. The Audit software to support Practitioners was developed and launched
  4. The opening of physical offices was completed for all branches bringing ICPAK brand and services closer to members
  5. A clear and transparent system of appointment to ICPAK organs and external positions and honours and awards was developed and implemented providing equal opportunities to all members
  6. We expanded the stakeholders’ engagements coverage to raise the profile of ICPAK
  7. We signed the MOU with AWAK to support growth opportunities for women Accountants
  8. We established collaboration working relationship with the Institute of Internal Auditors and drafted a collaborative framework MOU
  9. The whistleblower policy was developed and approved to protect primarily public sector CPAs
  10. The State of the Economy Report Framework was approved by Council

The journey to achieving greatness continues.

You see, you don’t set out to build a wall. You don’t say, ‘I’m going to build the biggest, baddest, greatest wall that’s ever been built. You don’t start there. You say, ‘I’m going to lay this brick as perfectly as a brick can be laid’. You do that every single day, and soon you have a wall.”
– Will Smith.

The Misses
  • In our pursuit for sustainability, we have continued to make the accountant dig deeper into their pockets to remain in good standing.
  • We are still not the go to authority on matters financial accountability.
  • There is still no single simple report that summarizes to ‘Wanjiku’ the state of financial accountability in Kenya across all sectors.
  • Not even one inquiry on any case or member completed and presented to council either for clearance or for forwarding to disciplinary committee.
  • Brand damage due to several cases of corruption, modified audit opinions and financial errors not responded to.

And this is where we will build our Manifesto for the Future from. You and I. Because it is all about YOU.
